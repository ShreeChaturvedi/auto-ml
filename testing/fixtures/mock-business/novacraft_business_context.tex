\section{Executive Summary}

NovaCraft is a mid-market B2B SaaS company providing cloud-based project management
and collaboration tools. Founded in 2018, the company serves approximately 2,500
business customers across 35 countries, with a product suite spanning four
subscription tiers (Free, Starter, Professional, Enterprise).

This document describes the business context, data assets, and analytical
opportunities available in the NovaCraft mock data package. The data is designed
to exercise a full AutoML pipeline: profiling, preprocessing, feature engineering,
model training, and evaluation.

\subsection{Key Business Metrics}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Total Customers:} $\sim$2,500 active and churned accounts
    \item \textbf{Active Rate:} $\sim$82\% of customers remain active
    \item \textbf{Subscription Records:} $\sim$3,200 (1.3 per customer avg.)
    \item \textbf{Support Tickets:} $\sim$6,800 lifetime tickets
    \item \textbf{Usage Tracking:} $\sim$10,700 monthly usage snapshots
    \item \textbf{Marketing Campaigns:} $\sim$500 campaigns (2022--2025)
\end{itemize}

\section{Business Context}

\subsection{Product \& Pricing}

NovaCraft offers a tiered SaaS model:

\begin{tabular}{llrl}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Tier} & \textbf{Target Segment} & \textbf{MRR Range} & \textbf{Mix} \\

    Starter      & Small teams            & \$29--99      & 30\% \\

    Enterprise   & Large organizations    & \$499--2,500+ & 25\% \\

Revenue is primarily driven by seat-based pricing with annual and monthly billing

\subsection{Customer Base}

Customers span 12 industries, with Technology, Healthcare, and Finance representing
the largest segments. Company sizes range from startups (1--10 employees) to large
enterprises (1,000+), with the majority in the 11--200 employee band.

Acquisition channels include organic search (25\%), paid search, social media,
referrals, direct signups, partner channels, and content marketing.

\subsection{Stakeholders}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Product Team} — Feature adoption, usage patterns, NPS analysis
    \item \textbf{Customer Success} — Churn prediction, health scoring, escalation risk
    \item \textbf{Sales} — MRR forecasting, upsell/cross-sell identification
    \item \textbf{Marketing} — Campaign ROI, channel attribution, lead quality
    \item \textbf{Support} — Resolution time optimization, agent performance, CSAT drivers

\section{Recommended Analyses}

\subsection{Customer Churn Prediction}

\textbf{Target:} \texttt{is\_active} (binary classification)

Join customers with aggregated usage metrics and support ticket counts.
Key features: tenure (months since signup), plan tier, support ticket frequency,
average satisfaction score, usage trends, and subscription changes.

\subsection{Revenue Forecasting}

\textbf{Target:} \texttt{mrr\_usd} (regression)

Predict monthly recurring revenue from customer profile, company size,
industry, usage intensity, and feature adoption metrics.

\subsection{Support Escalation Prediction}

\textbf{Target:} \texttt{escalated} (binary classification)

Use ticket metadata (category, priority, description length) combined with
customer history to predict which tickets will require escalation.

\subsection{Marketing Campaign Optimization}

\textbf{Target:} \texttt{conversions} (regression)

Model campaign conversion rates as a function of channel, budget allocation,
target industry, campaign duration, and historical performance.

\subsection{Cross-Table Feature Engineering}

The relational structure enables rich derived features:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Tickets per customer per month
    \item Average satisfaction trend over time
    \item Usage growth rate (month-over-month)
    \item Subscription upgrade/downgrade patterns
    \item Days since last support interaction
\end{itemize}
